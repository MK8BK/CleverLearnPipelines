Mainland Australia
==================

[Coordinates](/wiki/Geographic_coordinate_system "Geographic coordinate system"): [27°S 133°E﻿ / ﻿27°S 133°E﻿ / -27; 133](https://geohack.toolforge.org/geohack.php?pagename=Mainland_Australia&params=27_S_133_E_source:wikidata)

Main landmass of the Australian continent

This article is about the main landmass of the Australian continent. For the continent, see [Australia (continent)](/wiki/Australia_(continent) "Australia (continent)").

**Mainland Australia** is the main [landmass](/wiki/Landmass "Landmass") of the [Australian continent](/wiki/Australia_(continent) "Australia (continent)"), excluding the [Aru Islands](/wiki/Aru_Islands_Regency "Aru Islands Regency"), [New Guinea](/wiki/New_Guinea "New Guinea"), [Tasmania](/wiki/Tasmania "Tasmania"), and other [Australian offshore islands](/wiki/List_of_islands_of_Australia "List of islands of Australia"). The landmass also constitutes the [mainland](/wiki/Mainland "Mainland") of the territory governed by the [Commonwealth of Australia](/wiki/Australia "Australia"), and the term, along with **continental Australia**, can be used in a geographic sense to exclude surrounding [continental islands](/wiki/Island#Continental_islands "Island") and [external territories](/wiki/States_and_territories_of_Australia "States and territories of Australia"). Generally, the term is applied to the states of [New South Wales](/wiki/New_South_Wales "New South Wales"),[[a]](#cite_note-1) [Queensland](/wiki/Queensland "Queensland"), [South Australia](/wiki/South_Australia "South Australia"), [Victoria](/wiki/Victoria_(Australia) "Victoria (Australia)"), and [Western Australia](/wiki/Western_Australia "Western Australia"), as well as the [Australian Capital Territory](/wiki/Australian_Capital_Territory "Australian Capital Territory"), [Jervis Bay Territory](/wiki/Jervis_Bay_Territory "Jervis Bay Territory"), and [Northern Territory](/wiki/Northern_Territory "Northern Territory").

The term is typically used when referring to the relationship between Tasmania and the other Australian states,[[1]](#cite_note-2)[[2]](#cite_note-3) in that people not from Tasmania are referred to as mainlanders.[[3]](#cite_note-NMA-4) [Tasmania has been omitted](/wiki/Omission_of_Tasmania_from_maps_of_Australia "Omission of Tasmania from maps of Australia") on a number of occasions from maps of Australia, reinforcing the divide between Tasmania and the mainland. The [1982 Commonwealth Games](/wiki/1982_Commonwealth_Games "1982 Commonwealth Games") in [Brisbane](/wiki/Brisbane "Brisbane") left Tasmania off the map of Australia during the opening ceremony, as did the designs of the [Australian Swim Team](/wiki/Australian_Swim_Team "Australian Swim Team") uniform for the [2014 Commonwealth Games](/wiki/2014_Commonwealth_Games "2014 Commonwealth Games") in [Glasgow](/wiki/Glasgow "Glasgow").[[3]](#cite_note-NMA-4)

The land mass covers 7,591,608 km2 (2,931,136 sq mi), about 98.7% of the area of the country of Australia and 1.5% of [Earth](/wiki/Earth "Earth")'s surface.[[4]](#cite_note-5) It is sometimes described as an island, in which case it would be the [largest island by area](/wiki/List_of_islands_by_area "List of islands by area")–more than three times the size of [Greenland](/wiki/Greenland "Greenland").[[5]](#cite_note-6) Its population is about 25.9 million, 98% of Australia's total population. Mainland Australia has a variety of climatic regions, ranging from tropical rainforests and deserts to cool temperature rainforests to snow-covered mountains.[[6]](#cite_note-The_Australian_Continent-7) It is in these mainland regions that much of Australia's native [flora](/wiki/Flora_of_Australia "Flora of Australia") and [fauna](/wiki/Fauna_of_Australia "Fauna of Australia") can be found.

History
-------

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mainland_Australia&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: History")]

See also: [Natural history of Australia](/wiki/Natural_history_of_Australia "Natural history of Australia")

Early in the [Cretaceous](/wiki/Cretaceous "Cretaceous") period, 130 million years ago,[[7]](#cite_note-Australian_Separation-8) Australia (and [Antarctica](/wiki/Antarctica "Antarctica")) separated from a [supercontinent](/wiki/Supercontinent "Supercontinent") known as [Gondwana](/wiki/Gondwana "Gondwana"). Antarctica's separation from Australia began roughly 85 million years ago, at a rate of a few millimetres per year.[[8]](#cite_note-9)
It took over 30 million years (53 million years ago), toward the end of the [Palaeocene](/wiki/Paleocene "Paleocene") [epoch](/wiki/Epoch "Epoch"),[[7]](#cite_note-Australian_Separation-8) for Australia to fully separate from Antarctica and set course for where we see the two [continents](/wiki/Continent "Continent") today.

During the [last ice-age](/wiki/Last_Glacial_Period "Last Glacial Period") around 35,000 years ago,[[9]](#cite_note-10) sea levels (around Australia) dropped by 120 metres (390 ft), developing a continuous stretch of land between what is now [Papua New Guinea](/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea "Papua New Guinea"), Australia and [Tasmania](/wiki/Tasmania "Tasmania"). Over the next 6,000[[10]](#cite_note-11) years the ice gradually melted, increasing sea-levels, cutting off Papua New Guinea and Tasmania from mainland Australia.

### Prehistory

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mainland_Australia&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Prehistory")]

See also: [Aboriginal Australians](/wiki/Aboriginal_Australians "Aboriginal Australians")

It has been estimated that [Australian Aborigines](/wiki/Indigenous_Australians "Indigenous Australians") occupied mainland Australia up to 50,000 to 60,000[[11]](#cite_note-12) years ago, well before the last ice-age. Although an exact figure has not been decided on, numerous [DNA](/wiki/DNA "DNA") studies all confirm that Aboriginal Australians are one of the oldest living populations in the world[[12]](#cite_note-13) outside of [Africa](/wiki/Africa "Africa").
Prior to the arrival of [Europeans](/wiki/Europeans "Europeans") on the Australian mainland in the late 18th century, over 500[[13]](#cite_note-Our_People_-_Indigenous-14) different [clan](/wiki/Clan "Clan") groups (nations) each with unique cultures, beliefs and languages inhabited territories on the mainland. Currently, Indigenous people living on the mainland make up 2.4%[[13]](#cite_note-Our_People_-_Indigenous-14) of the total Australian population, encompassing over 250 [language](/wiki/Language "Language") groups.[[14]](#cite_note-IATSIS-15)

### European colonization

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mainland_Australia&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: European colonization")]

The first documented encounter of the Australian mainland was by [Willem Janszoon](/wiki/Willem_Janszoon "Willem Janszoon") in 1606, as he sailed around Australia's north coast arriving in present-day [Cape York, Queensland](/wiki/Cape_York_Peninsula "Cape York Peninsula").[[15]](#cite_note-16) These explorations led way to the term '[Terra Australis](/wiki/Terra_Australis "Terra Australis")', [Latin](/wiki/Latin "Latin") for South Land, which was hypothesised on the notion there must be land in the [Southern Hemisphere](/wiki/Southern_Hemisphere "Southern Hemisphere") to balance the land mass in the [Northern Hemisphere](/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere "Northern Hemisphere").[[16]](#cite_note-17) This encounter led several other [Dutch](/wiki/Dutch_people "Dutch people"), [Spanish](/wiki/Spaniards "Spaniards"), [Portuguese](/wiki/Portuguese_people "Portuguese people") and [British](/wiki/British_people "British people") journeyings to Terra Australis throughout the 17th and 18th century, progressively [mapping](/wiki/Mapping_(cartography) "Mapping (cartography)") what we now know to be as (mainland) Australia.
The most famous and well documented expedition of Australia occurred 164 years after Janszoon's journey, through British navigator and explorer [James Cook](/wiki/James_Cook "James Cook"). He documented the first interaction of the Eastern Australian coastline, and on 23 April 1770, Cook made the first recorded observation of Indigenous Australians[[17]](#cite_note-18) at nowadays [Brush Island](/wiki/Brush_Island "Brush Island"). This journey, coupled with numerous other reports, ultimately led the British to establish a [penal colony](/wiki/Penal_colony "Penal colony") in Australia, firstly at [Botany Bay](/wiki/Botany_Bay "Botany Bay").

Geography
---------

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mainland_Australia&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Geography")]

See also: [Geography of Australia](/wiki/Geography_of_Australia "Geography of Australia")

See also: [Climate of Australia](/wiki/Climate_of_Australia "Climate of Australia")

The Australian mainland's [geology](/wiki/Geology_of_Australia "Geology of Australia") and [climate](/wiki/Climate_of_Australia "Climate of Australia") is one of its defining features. Despite being surrounded by ocean, approximately 20% of the Australian mainland is classified as [desert](/wiki/Desert "Desert").[[6]](#cite_note-The_Australian_Continent-7) This is due to the extremely variable [rain](/wiki/Rain "Rain") patterns across the mainland. Toward the barren centre of the mainland, the rainfall pattern is concentric and sparse, whereas there is higher intensity rainfall toward the mainland's [tropics](/wiki/Tropics "Tropics") and coastal areas.
Australia lies on the middle of the [Indo-Australian tectonic plate](/wiki/Indo-Australian_Plate "Indo-Australian Plate")[[18]](#cite_note-19) and as a result is not influenced by any severe [tectonic activity](/wiki/Plate_tectonics "Plate tectonics"). Over millions of years, the shifting of these tectonic plates has seen the Australian mainland undergo many physical changes. Mountain ranges and seas have come and gone, and the forces of [weathering](/wiki/Weathering "Weathering") and [erosion](/wiki/Erosion "Erosion") have formed much of the mainlands current [topography](/wiki/Topography "Topography").[[19]](#cite_note-Geoactive-20)
These physical changes developed four primary [landform](/wiki/Landform "Landform") divisions spread throughout the mainland states and territories; the Coastal Plains, the [Eastern and Southern highlands](/wiki/South_Eastern_Highlands "South Eastern Highlands"), the Central Lowlands (and Deserts) and the [Western and Northern Plateaus](/wiki/Western_Plateau "Western Plateau") (and Basins).

### Coastal Plains

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mainland_Australia&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Coastal Plains")]

Along the Eastern seaboard of the mainland are the Coastal Plains; a narrow strip of land along the East coast of Australia from [Queensland to Victoria](/wiki/Eastern_states_of_Australia "Eastern states of Australia").[[20]](#cite_note-21) This division is host to some of the mainland's major cities, namely, [Brisbane](/wiki/Brisbane "Brisbane"), [Sydney](/wiki/Sydney "Sydney") and [Melbourne](/wiki/Melbourne "Melbourne"), due to its hospitable conditions. Some sources refer to the Coastal Plains as the entire coastline of the mainland, outspreading beyond the East Coast.[[19]](#cite_note-Geoactive-20)

### Eastern Highlands

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mainland_Australia&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Eastern Highlands")]

Approximately 100 km (62 mi) inland, the [Eastern Highlands](/wiki/South_Eastern_Highlands "South Eastern Highlands") (including the [Great Dividing Range](/wiki/Great_Dividing_Range "Great Dividing Range")) cover almost 10% of the mainland.[[21]](#cite_note-theaustralianenvironment.weebly.com-22) Incorporating several mountain ranges which extend 3,700 km (2,300 mi)[[22]](#cite_note-23) the Highlands run parallel to the east coast of the mainland; covering Queensland, New South Wales, the Australian Capital Territory and Victoria. The highlands were formed around 80 million years ago,[[23]](#cite_note-24) and are some of the oldest and most prominent in the world. This area is very rugged and primarily consists of a series of tablelands and plateaus. The highlands are much more prominent toward the south east of the mainland and form the Southern Highlands, which are located across (south) New South Wales, the Australian Capital Territory and Victoria. These Southern highlands [experience snowfall](/wiki/Snowy_Mountains "Snowy Mountains") for more than half the year during the winter due to their higher altitude.

### Central Lowlands

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mainland_Australia&action=edit&section=7 "Edit section: Central Lowlands")]

Occupying approximately 25% of the Australian mainland,[[21]](#cite_note-theaustralianenvironment.weebly.com-22) the Central Lowlands (and Deserts) are low-lying with an average height of under 200 metres (660 ft) above sea level. It is home to the [Sturt](/wiki/Sturt_Stony_Desert "Sturt Stony Desert") and [Simpson](/wiki/Simpson_Desert "Simpson Desert") deserts and closer to [Central Australia](/wiki/Central_Australia "Central Australia") lie some desert mountain ranges ([MacDonnell](/wiki/MacDonnell_Ranges "MacDonnell Ranges") and [Musgrave](/wiki/Musgrave_Ranges "Musgrave Ranges")). The Central Lowlands incorporates three primary [drainage basins](/wiki/Drainage_basins "Drainage basins"): in the North, the [Carpentaria Lowlands](/wiki/Gulf_of_Carpentaria "Gulf of Carpentaria"), [Lake Eyre](/wiki/Lake_Eyre "Lake Eyre") toward the centre and the [Murray-Darling Basin](/wiki/Murray-Darling_Basin "Murray-Darling Basin") down South.[[19]](#cite_note-Geoactive-20) The lowest point on the mainland is found in this division at Lake Eyre, at 50m below sea level.[[24]](#cite_note-Australian_Government-25)

### Western Plateau

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mainland_Australia&action=edit&section=8 "Edit section: Western Plateau")]

Approximately 144 to 65 million years ago ([Cretaceous](/wiki/Cretaceous "Cretaceous") period), there lay a great inland sea, stretching over one quarter of the mainland.[[25]](#cite_note-26) The evaporation of this ocean left behind what we now know as the Western Plateau. The [Western](/wiki/Western_Plateau "Western Plateau") and Northern Plateaus incorporate nearly all [Western Australia](/wiki/Western_Australia "Western Australia") and the [Northern Territory](/wiki/Northern_Territory "Northern Territory") and parts of [South Australia](/wiki/South_Australia "South Australia").
Over one-third of the mainland, the Plateaus are made of one stable block of [igneous](/wiki/Igneous "Igneous") and [metamorphic rock](/wiki/Metamorphic_rock "Metamorphic rock") which is up to 3.6 billion years old.[[21]](#cite_note-theaustralianenvironment.weebly.com-22) Despite this, the Plateau is a huge area of tablelands, averaging 500m above sea level in some parts.[[19]](#cite_note-Geoactive-20) It is home to [Uluru](/wiki/Uluru "Uluru"), [Kata Tjuta](/wiki/Kata_Tjuta "Kata Tjuta"), the [Finke River](/wiki/Finke_River "Finke River"), the [Victorian Desert](/wiki/Great_Victoria_Desert "Great Victoria Desert"), the [Great Sandy Desert](/wiki/Great_Sandy_Desert "Great Sandy Desert") and the [Gibson Desert](/wiki/Gibson_Desert "Gibson Desert").
Much of the Western Plateau receives minimal rainfall and thus appears red in colour. Conversely, the Northern Plateau experiences heavy rainfall throughout the [wet season](/wiki/Wet_season "Wet season") (November to April),[[24]](#cite_note-Australian_Government-25) due to its more tropical location compared to the Western Plateau.

Natural resources
-----------------

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mainland_Australia&action=edit&section=9 "Edit section: Natural resources")]

Australia's separation from the other continents resulted in a long period of [uplift](/wiki/Tectonic_uplift "Tectonic uplift"), [erosion](/wiki/Erosion "Erosion") and thick [terrigenous deposition](/wiki/Terrigenous_sediment "Terrigenous sediment") in [rift valleys](/wiki/Rift_valley "Rift valley").[[7]](#cite_note-Australian_Separation-8) It was these processes which over millions of years has led to the accumulation of Australia's main known [mineral](/wiki/Mineral "Mineral"), [oil](/wiki/Oil "Oil") and [gas](/wiki/Gas "Gas") resources. Most of Australia's oil and gas exploration occurs off the mainland. However, on the mainland, the abundance of minerals (and thus [metals](/wiki/Metals "Metals")) has formed the basis of Australia's dependence on [mining](/wiki/Mining "Mining");[[26]](#cite_note-27) [exporting](/wiki/Exporting "Exporting") much of these natural occurring resources overseas.

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mainland_Australia&action=edit&section=10 "Edit section: Mining")]

See also: [Mining in Australia](/wiki/Mining_in_Australia "Mining in Australia")

The Australian mainland is host to some of the world's largest open-cut and underground mines. Across the mainland states (besides the ACT), there are over 350 operating mines, producing 19 useful [rocks](/wiki/Rock_(geology) "Rock (geology)") and [minerals](/wiki/Minerals "Minerals"). ### Renewables

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mainland_Australia&action=edit&section=11 "Edit section: Renewables")]

See also: [Renewable energy in Australia](/wiki/Renewable_energy_in_Australia "Renewable energy in Australia")

The Australian mainland's various geology and climate, has led to the increased utilisation of renewable energy ([solar](/wiki/Solar_energy "Solar energy"), [wind](/wiki/Wind_power "Wind power") and [hydro](/wiki/Hydroelectricity "Hydroelectricity")) over recent decades.[[34]](#cite_note-Bulletin-35) In 2021, approximately 28.9% of the mainland's total electricity generation was from renewable energy sources with South Australia at 66.5% renewable energy generation, Victoria at 33.4%, Western Australia at 31.5%, New South Wales at 26.0% and Queensland at 19.6%.[[35]](#cite_note-36) The majority of this renewable energy was generated through [wind](/wiki/Wind_power "Wind power") and [solar](/wiki/Solar_energy "Solar energy"), proportionally contributing 35.9% and 38.3% respectively.

Politics
--------

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mainland_Australia&action=edit&section=12 "Edit section: Politics")]

See also: [Politics of Australia](/wiki/Politics_of_Australia "Politics of Australia")

Australian political decisions are typically deliberated and enacted within the limits of homeland (mainland Australia) soil. Despite Tasmania being one of Australia's six [British](/wiki/Great_Britain "Great Britain") establishment [colonies](/wiki/Colony "Colony"), [Tasmania](/wiki/Tasmania "Tasmania") has often been looked over due to the state's small size and general isolation. This is observed through the states and territories of mainland Australia representing 146 of the 151 [members in Parliament](/wiki/Parliament_of_Australia "Parliament of Australia").[[36]](#cite_note-37)

The 25 March [2023 New South Wales state election](/wiki/2023_New_South_Wales_state_election "2023 New South Wales state election") marked the second time in history that the [Australian Labor Party](/wiki/Australian_Labor_Party "Australian Labor Party") gained control of the entirety of Mainland Australia at the federal and mainland state levels simultaneously (leaving [Tasmania](/wiki/Tasmania "Tasmania") as the only state with a Liberal government), a feat that had last been achieved in 2007.[[37]](#cite_note-Slade_2023-38)[[38]](#cite_note-Bongiorno_2023-39) This would last until 24 August [2024 Northern Territory general election](/wiki/2024_Northern_Territory_general_election "2024 Northern Territory general election") when Labor lost Northern Territory to the [Country Liberal Party](/wiki/Country_Liberal_Party "Country Liberal Party") (CLP) opposition. Losing its first mainland state/Territory level.

The Liberal National opposition have never had this feat (control of the entirety of Mainland Australia at the federal and mainland state levels simultaneously) as of 2024.

### State formation

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mainland_Australia&action=edit&section=13 "Edit section: State formation")]

From 1788 to 1859, [Britain](/wiki/Great_Britain "Great Britain") established five colonies on the Australian mainland,[[39]](#cite_note-Mainland_Colonies-40) and one in [Tasmania](/wiki/Tasmania "Tasmania"). All six colonies were constitutionally connected to [Britain](/wiki/Great_Britain "Great Britain"), each with their own [parliament](/wiki/Parliament "Parliament") and [courts](/wiki/Courts "Courts"), with their respective [laws](/wiki/Laws "Laws") subject to the laws of [British Parliament](/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom "Parliament of the United Kingdom").

These colonies formed the six [states](/wiki/States_and_territories_of_Australia "States and territories of Australia") of Australia; [New South Wales](/wiki/New_South_Wales "New South Wales"), [Victoria](/wiki/Victoria_(Australia) "Victoria (Australia)"), [Western Australia](/wiki/Western_Australia "Western Australia"), [South Australia](/wiki/South_Australia "South Australia") (included the [Northern Territory](/wiki/Northern_Territory "Northern Territory") at this time), [Queensland](/wiki/Queensland "Queensland") and [Tasmania](/wiki/Tasmania "Tasmania"). In 1901, the six states were united as the [Commonwealth of Australia](/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Australia "Commonwealth of Australia"), under a new federal [constitution](/wiki/Constitution "Constitution").[[40]](#cite_note-Australian_Federation-41) In 1911, the Northern Territory was transferred from South Australia to the Commonwealth and formed a [territory](/wiki/Territory "Territory").[[39]](#cite_note-Mainland_Colonies-40)

### Capital selection

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Mainland_Australia&action=edit&section=14 "Edit section: Capital selection")]

See also: [Canberra](/wiki/Canberra "Canberra")

After [Federation](/wiki/Federation_of_Australia "Federation of Australia") in 1901,[[40]](#cite_note-Australian_Federation-41) it was always assumed the Australian [capital](/wiki/Capital_city "Capital city") would be on the [mainland](/wiki/Mainland "Mainland"). However, the location of the capital sparked years of negotiating between politicians in New South Wales and Victoria, the two most prominent states, who each wanted their capital, [Sydney](/wiki/Sydney "Sydney") and [Melbourne](/wiki/Melbourne "Melbourne") respectively, to become the new country's capital.

After years of debate to no avail, the two states decided on a compromise that met the requirements of the [Australian Constitution](/wiki/Constitution_of_Australia "Constitution of Australia"). A separate [territory](/wiki/Territory "Territory") (the [Australian Capital Territory](/wiki/Australian_Capital_Territory "Australian Capital Territory")) was to be established on land given by New South Wales, however it must be at least 100 miles (160 km) from Sydney.[[41]](#cite_note-42) This territory would contain the official capital of Australia, and [Parliament](/wiki/Parliament "Parliament") would sit in Melbourne until the city (now [Canberra](/wiki/Canberra "Canberra")) was built. In 1909, this compromise was agreed upon and was finally legislated in 1911.[[42]](#cite_note-43)

On 12 March 1913, [Canberra](/wiki/Canberra "Canberra") was officially named as the capital of Australia.[[43]](#cite_note-44) In 1915, [Jervis Bay](/wiki/Jervis_Bay "Jervis Bay") territory was added to the Australian Capital Territory to ensure the [Commonwealth](/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Australia "Commonwealth of Australia") had a [seaport](/wiki/Seaport "Seaport").[[44]](#cite_note-45)
