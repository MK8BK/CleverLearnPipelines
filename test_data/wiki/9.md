The **Lesser Sunda Islands** ([Indonesian](/wiki/Indonesian_language "Indonesian language"): *Kepulauan Sunda Kecil*, [Tetum](/wiki/Tetum_language "Tetum language"): *Illá Sunda ki'ik sirá*, [Balinese](/wiki/Balinese_language "Balinese language"): ᬓᬧᬸᬮᭀᬯᬦ᭄ᬲᬸᬦ᭄ᬤᬘᬾᬦᬶᬓ᭄, romanized: *Kapuloan Sunda cénik*), now known as **Nusa Tenggara Islands** ([Indonesian](/wiki/Indonesian_language "Indonesian language"): *Kepulauan Nusa Tenggara*, or "Southeast Islands"),[[1]](#cite_note-1) are an [archipelago](/wiki/Archipelago "Archipelago") in the [Indonesian archipelago](/wiki/Indonesian_archipelago "Indonesian archipelago"). Most of the Lesser Sunda Islands are located within the [Wallacea](/wiki/Wallacea "Wallacea") region, except for the [Bali](/wiki/Bali "Bali") province which is west of the [Wallace Line](/wiki/Wallace_Line "Wallace Line") and is within the [Sunda Shelf](/wiki/Sunda_Shelf "Sunda Shelf"). Together with the [Greater Sunda Islands](/wiki/Greater_Sunda_Islands "Greater Sunda Islands") to the west, they make up the [Sunda Islands](/wiki/Sunda_Islands "Sunda Islands"). The islands are part of a [volcanic arc](/wiki/Volcanic_arc "Volcanic arc"), the [Sunda Arc](/wiki/Sunda_Arc "Sunda Arc"), formed by [subduction](/wiki/Subduction "Subduction") along the [Sunda Trench](/wiki/Sunda_Trench "Sunda Trench") in the [Java Sea](/wiki/Java_Sea "Java Sea"). In 1930 the population was 3,460,059;[[2]](#cite_note-2) today over 17 million people live on the islands. Etymologically, Nusa Tenggara means "Southeast Islands" from the words of *nusa* which means 'island' from [Old Javanese language](/wiki/Old_Javanese_language "Old Javanese language") and *tenggara* means 'southeast'.

The main Lesser Sunda Islands are, from west to east: [Bali](/wiki/Bali "Bali"), [Lombok](/wiki/Lombok "Lombok"), [Sumbawa](/wiki/Sumbawa "Sumbawa"), [Flores](/wiki/Flores "Flores"), [Sumba](/wiki/Sumba "Sumba"), [Savu](/wiki/Savu "Savu"), [Rote](/wiki/Rote_Island "Rote Island"), [Timor](/wiki/Timor "Timor"), [Atauro](/wiki/Atauro "Atauro"), [Alor archipelago](/wiki/Alor_archipelago "Alor archipelago"), [Barat Daya Islands](/wiki/Barat_Daya_Islands "Barat Daya Islands"), and [Tanimbar Islands](/wiki/Tanimbar_Islands "Tanimbar Islands"). Apart from the eastern half of [Timor](/wiki/Timor "Timor") island and [Atauro](/wiki/Atauro "Atauro") island which constitute the nation of [Timor Leste](/wiki/Timor_Leste "Timor Leste"), all the other islands are part of [Indonesia](/wiki/Indonesia "Indonesia").

Geology
-------

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Lesser_Sunda_Islands&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Geology")]

The Lesser Sunda Islands consist of two geologically distinct archipelagos.[[3]](#cite_note-Audley-Charles-3) The northern archipelago, which includes [Bali](/wiki/Bali "Bali"), [Lombok](/wiki/Lombok "Lombok"), [Sumbawa](/wiki/Sumbawa "Sumbawa"), [Flores](/wiki/Flores "Flores") and [Wetar](/wiki/Wetar "Wetar"), is volcanic in origin. A number of these [volcanoes](/wiki/Volcano "Volcano"), like [Mount Rinjani](/wiki/Mount_Rinjani "Mount Rinjani") on Lombok, are still active while others, such as Ilikedeka on Flores, are extinct. The northern archipelago began to be formed during the [Pliocene](/wiki/Pliocene "Pliocene"), about 15 million years ago, as a result of the collision between the [Australian](/wiki/Indo-Australian_Plate "Indo-Australian Plate") and the [Asian plates](/wiki/Eurasian_Plate "Eurasian Plate").[[3]](#cite_note-Audley-Charles-3) The islands of the southern archipelago, including [Sumba](/wiki/Sumba "Sumba"), [Timor](/wiki/Timor "Timor") and [Babar](/wiki/Babar_Islands "Babar Islands"), are non-volcanic and appear to belong to the [Australian plate](/wiki/Indo-Australian_Plate "Indo-Australian Plate").[[4]](#cite_note-4) The geology and ecology of the northern archipelago share similar history, characteristics, and processes with the southern [Maluku Islands](/wiki/Maluku_Islands "Maluku Islands"), which continue the same island arc to the east.

There is a long history of geological study of these regions since [Indonesian colonial times](/wiki/Dutch_East_Indies "Dutch East Indies"); however, the geological formation and progression is not fully understood, and theories of the geological evolution of the islands changed extensively during the last decades of the 20th century.[[5]](#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMonkFretesReksodiharjo-Lilley19969-5)

Lying at the collision of two [tectonic plates](/wiki/Plate_tectonics "Plate tectonics"), the Lesser Sunda Islands comprise some of the most geologically complex and active regions in the world. The province of [Bali](/wiki/Bali "Bali") is the only part of Nusa Tenggara located on the [Sunda Shelf](/wiki/Sunda_Shelf "Sunda Shelf") and that is not within the [Wallacea](/wiki/Wallacea "Wallacea") region and that is west of the [Wallace Line](/wiki/Wallace_Line "Wallace Line").[[5]](#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMonkFretesReksodiharjo-Lilley19969-5)

There are a number of volcanoes located on the Lesser Sunda Islands.[[6]](#cite_note-6)

Ecology
-------

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Lesser_Sunda_Islands&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Ecology")]

The Lesser Sunda Islands differ from the large islands of [Java](/wiki/Java "Java") or [Sumatra](/wiki/Sumatra "Sumatra") in consisting of many small islands, sometimes divided by deep [oceanic trenches](/wiki/Oceanic_trench "Oceanic trench"). Movement of flora and fauna between islands is limited, leading to the evolution of a high rate of localized species, most famously the [Komodo dragon](/wiki/Komodo_dragon "Komodo dragon").[[5]](#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMonkFretesReksodiharjo-Lilley19969-5) As described by [Alfred Wallace](/wiki/Alfred_Wallace "Alfred Wallace") in *[The Malay Archipelago](/wiki/The_Malay_Archipelago "The Malay Archipelago")*, the [Wallace Line](/wiki/Wallace_Line "Wallace Line") passes between Bali and Lombok, along the deep waters of the [Lombok Strait](/wiki/Lombok_Strait "Lombok Strait") which formed a water barrier even when lower sea levels linked the now-separated islands and landmasses on either side. The islands east of the Lombok Strait are part of [Wallacea](/wiki/Wallacea "Wallacea"), and are thus characterised by a blend of wildlife of [Asian](/wiki/Asia "Asia") and [Australasian](/wiki/Australasia "Australasia") origin in this region.[[7]](#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMonkFretesReksodiharjo-Lilley19964-7) Asian species predominate in the Lesser Sundas: [Weber's Line](/wiki/Max_Carl_Wilhelm_Weber "Max Carl Wilhelm Weber"), which marks the boundary between the parts of Wallacea with mainly Asian and Australasian species respectively, runs to the east of the group. These islands have the driest climate in Indonesia, and [tropical dry broadleaf forests](/wiki/Tropical_and_subtropical_dry_broadleaf_forests "Tropical and subtropical dry broadleaf forests") are predominant, in contrast to the [tropical moist forests](/wiki/Tropical_and_subtropical_moist_broadleaf_forests "Tropical and subtropical moist broadleaf forests") that prevail in most of Indonesia.

### Ecoregions

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Lesser_Sunda_Islands&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Ecoregions")]

The Lesser Sunda Islands are divided among six ecoregions:[[8]](#cite_note-8)

* The [Eastern Java-Bali rain forests](/wiki/Eastern_Java-Bali_rain_forests "Eastern Java-Bali rain forests") and [Eastern Java-Bali montane rain forests](/wiki/Eastern_Java-Bali_montane_rain_forests "Eastern Java-Bali montane rain forests") cover [Bali](/wiki/Bali "Bali"), which is the only of the Lesser Sunda Islands in the [Indomalayan realm](/wiki/Indomalayan_realm "Indomalayan realm"), and not part of Wallacea. Bali was once attached to the Asian continent (see [Sundaland](/wiki/Sundaland "Sundaland")), and home to large Asiatic mammals like [Asian elephants](/wiki/Asian_elephant "Asian elephant") and the extinct [Bali tiger](/wiki/Bali_tiger "Bali tiger").
* The [Lesser Sundas deciduous forests](/wiki/Lesser_Sundas_deciduous_forests "Lesser Sundas deciduous forests") include the northern chain of islands, from Lombok and Sumbawa east to Flores and Alor. The higher slopes of the islands contain forests of tall *[Podocarpus](/wiki/Podocarpus "Podocarpus")* conifers and [Engelhardias](/wiki/Engelhardia "Engelhardia") with an undergrowth of [lianas](/wiki/Liana "Liana"), [epiphytes](/wiki/Epiphyte "Epiphyte"), and orchids such as *[Corybas](/wiki/Corybas_(plant) "Corybas (plant)")*, *[Corymborkis](/wiki/Corymborkis "Corymborkis")*, and *[Malaxis](/wiki/Malaxis "Malaxis")* (adder's mouth), while the coastal plains were originally [savanna](/wiki/Savanna "Savanna") grasses such as the savanna with [Borassus flabellifer](/wiki/Borassus_flabellifer "Borassus flabellifer") palm trees on the coasts of Komodo, Rincah and Flores. Although most of the vegetation on these islands is dry forest there are patches of rainforest on these islands too, especially in lowland areas and riverbanks on Komodo, and there is a particular area of dry thorny forest on the southeast coast of Lombok. Thorn trees used to be more common in coastal areas of the islands but have largely been cleared. These islands are home to unique species including seventeen endemic birds (of the 273 birds found on the islands). The endemic mammals are the endangered Flores shrew *([Suncus mertensi](/wiki/Suncus_mertensi "Suncus mertensi"))*, the vulnerable Komodo rat *([Komodomys rintjanus](/wiki/Komodomys_rintjanus "Komodomys rintjanus"))*, and Lombok flying fox *([Pteropus lombocensis](/wiki/Pteropus_lombocensis "Pteropus lombocensis"))*, Sunda long-eared bat *([Nyctophilus heran](/wiki/Nyctophilus_heran "Nyctophilus heran"))* while the carnivorous Komodo dragon, which at three metres long and ninety kilograms in weight is the world's largest lizard, is found on Komodo, Rincah, Gili Motang, and the coast of northwestern Flores.
* The [Sumba deciduous forests](/wiki/Sumba_deciduous_forests "Sumba deciduous forests") ecoregion includes [Sumba](/wiki/Sumba "Sumba").
* The [Timor and Wetar deciduous forests](/wiki/Timor_and_Wetar_deciduous_forests "Timor and Wetar deciduous forests") ecoregion includes Timor, [Wetar](/wiki/Wetar "Wetar") (actually in [Maluku](/wiki/Maluku_(province) "Maluku (province)") Province), [Rote](/wiki/Rote_Island "Rote Island"), and [Savu](/wiki/Savu "Savu").
* The more humid [Banda Sea Islands moist deciduous forests](/wiki/Banda_Sea_Islands_moist_deciduous_forests "Banda Sea Islands moist deciduous forests") ecoregion includes the Barat Daya Islands (except for Wetar), the [Tanimbar Islands](/wiki/Tanimbar_Islands "Tanimbar Islands"), and the [Kai Islands](/wiki/Kai_Islands "Kai Islands").

### Threats and preservation

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Lesser_Sunda_Islands&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Threats and preservation")]

More than half of the original vegetation of the islands has been cleared for planting of rice and other crops, for settlement and by consequent forest fires. Only Sumbawa now contains a large area of intact natural forest, while Komodo, Rincah and Padar are now protected as [Komodo National Park](/wiki/Komodo_National_Park "Komodo National Park").

While many ecological problems affect both small islands and large landmasses, small islands suffer their particular problems and are highly exposed to external forces. Development pressures on small islands are increasing, although their effects are not always anticipated. Although Indonesia is richly endowed with natural resources, the resources of the small islands of Nusa Tenggara are limited and specialised; furthermore human resources in particular are limited.[[9]](#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMonkFretesReksodiharjo-Lilley19961-9)

General observations[[10]](#cite_note-10) about small islands that can be applied to Nusa Tenggara include:[[9]](#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMonkFretesReksodiharjo-Lilley19961-9)

* A higher proportion of the landmass will be affected by [volcanic activity](/wiki/List_of_volcanoes_in_Indonesia "List of volcanoes in Indonesia"), earthquakes, landslips, and cyclone damage;
* Climates are more likely to be [maritime](/wiki/Maritime_climate "Maritime climate") influenced;
* [Catchment areas](/wiki/Drainage_basin "Drainage basin") are smaller and degree of [erosion](/wiki/Erosion "Erosion") higher;
* A higher proportion of the landmass is made up of coastal areas;
* A higher degree of environmental specialisation, including a higher proportion of [endemic](/wiki/Endemism "Endemism") species in an overall [depauperate](/wiki/Depauperate "Depauperate") community;
* Societies having developed in relative isolation may retain a strong sense of culture;
* Small island populations are more likely to be affected by economic migration.

Administration
--------------

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Lesser_Sunda_Islands&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Administration")]

The Lesser Sundas comprise many islands stretching east of [Java](/wiki/Java "Java"), most of which are part of [Indonesia](/wiki/Indonesia "Indonesia") and from 1945 were administered (apart from the easternmost islands which have been always administered as part of [Maluku](/wiki/Maluku_(province) "Maluku (province)") Province) as the Lesser Sunda Islands (*Sunda Kecil*) Province of Indonesia, later called Nusa Tenggara. In 1958 this was split into three new provinces, as the [provinces](/wiki/Provinces_of_Indonesia "Provinces of Indonesia") of [Bali](/wiki/Bali "Bali"), [West Nusa Tenggara](/wiki/West_Nusa_Tenggara "West Nusa Tenggara") and [East Nusa Tenggara](/wiki/East_Nusa_Tenggara "East Nusa Tenggara").

The eastern half of [Timor](/wiki/Timor "Timor") Island is the separate nation of [East Timor](/wiki/East_Timor "East Timor") (officially Timor Leste}.
