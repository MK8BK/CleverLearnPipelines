**CentraleSupélec** (French pronunciation: [[sɑ̃tʁalsypelɛk]](/wiki/Help:IPA/French "Help:IPA/French"), **CS**) is one of the most prestigious and selective [grandes écoles](/wiki/Grandes_%C3%A9coles "Grandes écoles") in [France](/wiki/France "France") and is a member of the [graduate engineering school](/wiki/Graduate_school "Graduate school") of [Paris-Saclay University](/wiki/Paris-Saclay_University "Paris-Saclay University") in [Gif-sur-Yvette](/wiki/Gif-sur-Yvette "Gif-sur-Yvette"), [France](/wiki/France "France"). It was established on 1 January 2015, as a result of a strategic merger between two prestigious [grandes écoles](/wiki/Grandes_%C3%A9coles "Grandes écoles") in France, [École Centrale Paris](/wiki/%C3%89cole_Centrale_Paris "École Centrale Paris") and [Supélec](/wiki/Sup%C3%A9lec "Supélec").[[1]](#cite_note-1) CentraleSupélec is also considered to be among the best engineering schools of France and most elite institutions in Europe, with an admission rate of 6 to 8% in 2019. [[2]](#cite_note-2) It is one of the constituent members of [Paris-Saclay University](/wiki/Paris-Saclay_University "Paris-Saclay University").[[3]](#cite_note-3)

It is a key founding member of the [Paris-Saclay University](/wiki/Paris-Saclay_University "Paris-Saclay University"), the [TIME](/wiki/Top_Industrial_Managers_for_Europe "Top Industrial Managers for Europe") (Top Industrial Managers for Europe) network and also the [CESAER](/wiki/CESAER "CESAER") association of European engineering schools.

History
-------

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=CentraleSup%C3%A9lec&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: History")]

### École Centrale des Arts et Manufactures

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=CentraleSup%C3%A9lec&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: École Centrale des Arts et Manufactures")]

See also: [École Centrale Paris](/wiki/%C3%89cole_Centrale_Paris "École Centrale Paris")

École Centrale Paris was founded in 1829 on a private initiative by [Alphonse Lavallée](/wiki/Alphonse_Lavall%C3%A9e "Alphonse Lavallée"), who financed its creation and became its first president, and three distinguished scientists: [Eugène Peclet](/wiki/Jean_Claude_Eug%C3%A8ne_P%C3%A9clet "Jean Claude Eugène Péclet"), [Jean-Baptiste Dumas](/wiki/Jean-Baptiste_Dumas "Jean-Baptiste Dumas"), and [Théodore Olivier](http://www-groups.dcs.st-and.ac.uk/~history/Printonly/Olivier.html). The founding vision was to promote the development of 'industrial science', or practical applications of recent major scientific discoveries, and to educate multidisciplinary engineers to lead the emerging industrial sector. The institution was offered to the French [state](/wiki/Sovereign_state "Sovereign state") in 1857.

Initially located in the Hôtel de Juigné (now Hôtel Salé and home to the [Musée Picasso](/wiki/Mus%C3%A9e_Picasso "Musée Picasso")), it was transferred to rue Montgolfier in 1884. It stayed there until 1969, when it relocated to [Châtenay-Malabry](/wiki/Ch%C3%A2tenay-Malabry "Châtenay-Malabry"), near the [Parc de Sceaux](/wiki/Parc_de_Sceaux "Parc de Sceaux"). It moved to its current location in 2015.

### École Supérieure d'Electricité

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=CentraleSup%C3%A9lec&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: École Supérieure d'Electricité")]

See also: [Supélec](/wiki/Sup%C3%A9lec "Supélec")

Supélec was founded in 1894 by [Eleuthère Mascart](/wiki/Eleuth%C3%A8re_Mascart "Eleuthère Mascart"), a prominent academic who was elected as a Perpetual Member and Secretary of the *Académie des Sciences* and Foreign Member of the British Royal Society. He was also a professor at the *Collège de France*, and recipient of the Bordin Prize in 1866 as well as the Grand prix de l'Académie des sciences in 1874.
Supélec was created in order to educate engineers for the then booming electrical industry.

Supélec was originally based in Rue de Staël, 15th district of Paris. It was then relocated to [Malakoff](/wiki/Malakoff,_Hauts-de-Seine "Malakoff, Hauts-de-Seine") close to Porte de Vanves, and Gif-sur-Yvette in 1975, where the main campus is currently located. A second campus was established in [Rennes](/wiki/Rennes "Rennes") in 1972 and a third in [Metz](/wiki/Metz "Metz") in 1985.

### CentraleSupélec Alliance

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=CentraleSup%C3%A9lec&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: CentraleSupélec Alliance")]

Since December 2008, a strategic alliance was announced between the two schools, known today through the joint name of CentraleSupélec. [Hervé Biausser](/wiki/Herv%C3%A9_Biausser "Hervé Biausser") became the director in 2013, 1 September, while keeping the directorship of [Centrale Paris](/wiki/Centrale_Paris "Centrale Paris").
CentraleSupélec now has three campuses in France: Gif-sur-Yvette, Metz and Rennes.

### Part of the Paris-Saclay University

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=CentraleSup%C3%A9lec&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Part of the Paris-Saclay University")]

See also: [Paris-Saclay University](/wiki/Paris-Saclay_University "Paris-Saclay University")

Since 2008, [École Centrale Paris](/wiki/%C3%89cole_Centrale_Paris "École Centrale Paris") and [Supélec](/wiki/Sup%C3%A9lec "Supélec") have participated in the creation of the campus of the [Paris-Saclay](/wiki/Paris-Saclay "Paris-Saclay") research cluster. The [Paris-Saclay University](/wiki/Paris-Saclay_University "Paris-Saclay University") is a French research university, which aims to become the top university of continental Europe in the [ARWU](/wiki/ARWU "ARWU") rankings.

Since October 2023, the school has been a partner of [IPSA](/wiki/Institut_polytechnique_des_sciences_avanc%C3%A9es "Institut polytechnique des sciences avancées") for double degrees in [aerospace](/wiki/Aerospace "Aerospace").[[4]](#cite_note-4)

The [Paris-Saclay University](/wiki/Paris-Saclay_University "Paris-Saclay University") was ranked 14th in the world in the 2020 [Academic Ranking of World Universities](/wiki/Academic_Ranking_of_World_Universities "Academic Ranking of World Universities") (ARWU) ranking. In subject rankings, it was placed first in the world for [Mathematics](/wiki/Paris-Saclay_Faculty_of_Sciences "Paris-Saclay Faculty of Sciences") and 9th in the world for [Physics](/wiki/Paris-Saclay_Faculty_of_Sciences "Paris-Saclay Faculty of Sciences") (1st in Europe), as well as receiving a top 25 place for [Medicine](/wiki/Paris-Saclay_Medical_School "Paris-Saclay Medical School") and [Agriculture](/wiki/AgroParisTech "AgroParisTech").[[5]](#cite_note-5)

### Admission

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=CentraleSup%C3%A9lec&action=edit&section=7 "Edit section: Admission")]

The majority of students admitted to the engineer's degree program are selected among candidates from French [preparatory classes](/wiki/Classes_pr%C3%A9paratoires "Classes préparatoires"), which is usually a two to three-year post-secondary program with a focus on certain subjects such as Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, Engineering Sciences, and Industrial Sciences. CentraleSupélec offers 142 places in the Mathematics and Physics program (MP), 88 places in the Physics and Chemistry program (PC), 93 places in the Physics and Engineering Sciences program (PSI), 10 places in the Physics and Technology program (PT), and 10 places in the Technology and Industrial Sciences program (TSI). The acceptance rate is 8% for MP students, 6% for PC students and 7% for PSI students.[[6]](#cite_note-6) The school also recruits top students from overseas partner universities as well as some graduates of French public universities to join the engineer's degree program.

Before 2018, students at CentraleSupélec were admitted to either the Centralien or Supélec engineer's degree programs (but not both), and consequently students enrolled before this year receive the academic degree bearing the name of their respective engineer program upon graduation.[[7]](#cite_note-7) On the other hand, all engineering students admitted in 2018 and onward are placed in a 'unified' engineer program and graduate with the same academic degree
